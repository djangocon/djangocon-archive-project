Django security clinic

Erik Romijn is well-known for his work for his talks and work on Django security. He's running a clinic to help you secure your Django site.

Note: this clinic will run alongside the conference sprints. It’s free to all conference attendees.

About the clinic

My talk on security for Django developers may have left you with questions about your own projects.

Perhaps you need a bit of help to fix some of the issues raised in my session, or you have concerns about other areas in your work.

In this workshop, we’ll work on such issues together.

Please bring your questions, your concerns and your code, and all together we’ll think about how we can gauge whether something is an issue, how serious it is, and how to resolve it within the limitations we’re presented with. Experiences like that will help you discover other issues in the future, and work out what to do, on your own.

Perhaps we’ll even try to exploit an actual issue, circumstances permitting (all within ethical and legal boundaries).

This workshop runs on your questions, your concerns and your code, so please bring something along.

About Erik Romijn

Erik’s a member of the Django core development team. One of his missions is to make security easier for Django developers who aren’t security professionals. He has written and given talks on practical security steps, and runs Erik's Pony Checkup, an automated security service for Django sites.

He lives in Amsterdam, where he’s part of DashCare. He’s the Chair of the Dutch Django Association and organises events such as its annual sprint. He was also one of the organisers of Django: Under the Hood in 2014.
