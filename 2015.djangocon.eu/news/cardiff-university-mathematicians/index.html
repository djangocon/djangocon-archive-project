Cardiff University mathematicians at DjangoCon

This year’s DjangoCon comes to you… from the School of Mathematics at Cardiff University!

This year’s DjangoCon has a number of participants from Cardiff University’s School of Mathematics.

Three members of the organising committee are from the School. Vince Knight, our Treasurer, is a lecturer in the School, while our Secretary Jason Young and Geraint Palmer are doctoral students (who both use Python/Django in their research).

Professor Tim Phillips, Head of the School, has generously agreed to sponsor tickets for three first-year students: Anna Clancy, Conor Selwood and Rhiannon Titcomb.

Rhiannon is giving a talk on the open day: she will be discussing the use and mathematics of Bezier curves.

Dafydd Evans, also a lecturer in the School, will also be speaking at the event: he will be presenting his use of Django in the development of a mathematics teaching resources delivery tool.

You can also expect a lightning talk from Vince on how Python powers this Django conference.

Python has been taught to all first year students for the past two years at Cardiff and many students choose to use Django for group projects.

It’s wonderful to have the involvement of the School of Mathematics and its mathematicians in this year’s event.
