How are we doing?

We set ourselves some ambitious targets for this DjangoCon. With 12 weeks to go, how close are we to the goals we set?

Tickets

We wanted this DjangoCon to be as affordable as possible. In fact, early-bird tickets will account for about two-thirds of all tickets sold, and even the full-price tickets for the event are extremely reasonably priced.

Ticket are selling extremely well and we expect to sell out completely well in advance of the conference. Don't leave it too late to get yours.

One thing we’re very happy about is that so far about:

  • two-thirds of our conference attendees will support the open day
  • three-quarters will stay on for the first day of sprints/workshops/clinics
  • two-thirds will stay on for the final day

This is unprecedented for a DjangoCon, and means that at this rate, we will have 260 people at the sprints.

We’re also pleased with the response to our donation tickets, and the fact that so many people in the community are willing to help others.

Programme

Our programme's looking really exciting already, even before the deadline for submissions closes. We have more tutorials and clinics in place than any previous DjangoCon, with more still to come, and have received many excellent talk proposals.

Accessibility

We promised an accessible event: we have checked with venues for accessibility provisions, contracted live text transcription services for talks, and we’re checking with attendees who mentioned accessibility requirements to ensure that those needs are met.

Diversity

We are determined that this will be the most diverse DjangoCon ever.

It almost certainly will be, but we’re not close to achieving the hoped-for diversity of speakers and participants - this DjangoCon will still be very white, male and western.

Tickets are still on sale though and there’s still just over a week left in which to submit a talk proposal or application for financial assistance, so perhaps there will be a final rush that helps balance the numbers more satisfactorily.

On the other hand we are pleased with the diversity initiatives that we have in place. Attendees are bringing their children to the free crèche, several speakers have taken advantage of the speaker mentor programme, Django Girls will be at the event in force, and our open day is going to help address a new audience.

You can help

If you can help us achieve our aim of a much more diverse conference - by encouraging friends and colleagues to sign up for talks or tickets, by signing up yourself, by sharing details of our initiatives - we’ll be most grateful to you.

Social responsibility

We’re pleased with the response we’ve had from attendees and sponsors to our social responsibility policy, and that we have been able to apply it successfully to aspects of the conference from conference meals to attendees’ gifts.

We’ll have more news on the subject soon, too.

Sponsorship

We’re a little behind our targets still, but hope that more sponsors are still to come on board. We’re disappointed that so far, we have not secured a single sponsor from Wales.

Thanks!

We’re very grateful to the Django community for its support, especially for some of our more ambitious or demanding aims. Where we’ve been successful in our efforts towards them, it’s thanks to the fact that people in the community share them too.
