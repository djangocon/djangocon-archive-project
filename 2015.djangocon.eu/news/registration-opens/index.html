Registration opens

Registration for tickets and the call for proposals are now open, with a special one-month window exclusive to members of under-represented groups.

We want more female attendees and speakers than ever before at this DjangoCon. We want more non-white and non-western people. We want a wider and more even distribution of ages. We want the people who don’t usually get to attend conferences, because they have children to look after or accessibility needs that make it more difficult. We want the people who have never been to a DjangoCon before.

We’re very keen that this DjangoCon should be the most diverse ever, and we’re going to do all we can to achieve that by making it attractive not just to the kind of people who usually come to DjangoCons, but also the ones who don’t.

For more on what we’re doing to help achieve this aim, see our diversity, accessibility and code of conduct policies and initiatives.

“Minority-only registration” (we couldn’t think of a better term) is available now. Everyone else will have to wait another month, for general registration (it won’t hurt them; they’ll still be able to get the same tickets at the same prices).

The same goes for the call for proposals: it’s open now, but only for members of under-represented groups until the 1st of February.

So, just for once, the first shall be last and the last shall be first.

If you’re a member of a conference-attending minority, you’re cordially invited to be first, and everyone who takes up that invitation will be ensuring that everyone else who does is in less of a minority.

Who’s ‘under-represented’?

If you’ve been to a conference like this before and found yourself amongst a few hundred people who seemed to be very similar to you, then you’re not in a minority.

On the other hand, if you’re not male, western, white, aged between 20 and 40 and able-bodied, or if you’ve never been to a conference before, or if you have family to look after so find it hard to go to conferences: you qualify as ‘under-represented’. In which case, please do make use of this invitation to get your registration in first.

Note that we won’t be checking anyone’s credentials who registers under this policy. We trust attendees to respect the policy and act fairly.

Donation and Diversity tickets

As well as buying your own ticket, you can make a donation that will be used to help support an attendee on a lower budget, or purchase a ticket on behalf of an attendee who could not otherwise afford it. Supported tickets and financial assistance will be allocated by an awards committee of the Django Software Foundation.
